标题

Getting girlhood right

使女性获得权力

副标题

Girls are doing better than even. Don't let the pandemic stymie them.

  • stymie：v. 阻止

女孩做的比现在好， 不要让新冠疫情大流行阻止她们做的更好。

全文

For much of human history and in many places, girls were considered property. Or, at best, subordinate people, required to obey their fathers until the day they had to obeying their husbends. Few people thought it worthwhile to educate them. Even fewer imagined that a girl could grow up to govern Germany, run the IMF or invent a vaccine.
In most of the world that vision of girlhood now seem not merely old-fasion but unimaginably remote. In much of the rich world parents treat their daughters as well as do their sons, and invest as much in their future. In field after field girls caught up with boys. Globally, young women number now outnumber young men at university. The speed of change has been blistering. Fifty years ago, only 49% of primery-school-age girls in low-middle-income countries were in school, compared with 71% of boys; today the shared of both is about 90%. In 1998 only half of secondery-school-age girls were enrolled; today two-third are. Over the same period rates of illiteracy fell from one five young women aged 15-24 to one in ten, bringing them roughly on a par with young men.
Girl baby are more want than ever before. Parents in some countries prefer them. Even in place, such as China, where the sex-selective abortion of girl fetuses has been rife, it is often become less so. Girls are also less likely to married off in childhood. In 1995 almost six in ten girls in South Asia were hitched before reaching 18; that ratio has fallen by half. Around the world, it has fell from one in four to one in five.
Girls are healthier, too. Compared with the mid-1990s, they become sexually active later and are more likely to use contraception.Rates of teenage pregnancy has fallen by a quarter globally and two-thirds in South Asia and North America. Girls are less likely to suffer female genital mutilation——and object to this horrific tradition more vocally. Whereas in 2000 just 27% of women and girls in the most-affected region said it should be banned, today 54% do. When societies handle girlhood well, the konck-on effect are astounding. A girl who finishes secondary school is less likely to become a child bride or a teenage mother. Education boosts earning power and widens choices, so she is less likely to be poor or to suffer domestic abuse. She will earn almost twice as much as a girl without schooling.
And she will pass on a smorgasbord of advantages to her offspring. She will have fewer childen, and invest more in them. They will be less likely to die in infancy, or to grow up stunted physically or mentally. She will read to them more, and help them with their homework. All this means that they will learn more, and earn more as adults. A recent stady by Citigroup and Plan International estimated that, if a group of emerging economies ensured that 100% of their girls complete secondary school, it could lead to a lasting boost to their GDP of 10% by 2030.

Because the benefits of nurturing girls are so large, it is a scandal that some countries have still failed to grasp them. Less than half the girl in South Asia, the Middle East or Africa have access to the contraception that they may want. Only one girl of three south of the Sahara finishes her secondary education. And although rates of child marriage have fallen by half in South Asia, they have fallen by less than that Africa (which now has the heighest rate in the world) and have remained stagnant in Latin America and the Caribbean.
The covid-19 pandemic could hobble progress for girl in poor countries. During previous disasters, they have often suffered most. When Ebola forced west African schools to close in 2014, many girls dropped out, never went back and ended up pregnant or as child labourers. UNICEF warns that something similar could happen in covid-19——but on a large scale. Studies suggests that in the next decade 13m child marriages that would have been averted may to go ahead, and an extra 2m girls may have their genital cut.

The risk of regression is real. So it is crutial that, even if governments of poor countries have to tighten their belt, they priorities spending on education and girls. Donors should help, too. And polities should joined up. Persuading girls to stay in school longer is not only a way to teach them maths; it is also a chance to vaccinate them and teach them about birth control, consent and self-assertion. It can even be an opportunity to advise parents about the downsides of child marriage.
Adolescence is a crucial juncture for girls. It is when many healthy problem emerge or are averted; and many social ones, too, from truancy to self-harm. Only recently has this phase been recognised as the most important for brain development after infancy. Get it right and brillion of girls will have a better shot at fulfilling their potential. Get it wrong and they will live poorer, shorter lives, less able to stand up for themselves, more vulnerable to coercion, and more like to pass these disadvantages on to the next generation. So, get girlhood right.

在这个世界上，女孩的眼界似乎不仅仅是旧时尚的，而且是难以想象的遥远。在这个世界上富有的人对待他们的女儿如同对待他们的儿子一样，并且投资在他们女儿的身上的投资和投资在儿子身上的投资一样多。在一个又一个领域，女孩已经追上了男孩。现在，在全球的大学里面女孩的数量已经超过了男孩的数量。这种改变的速率已经变得非常快了。在五十年前的中低等收入国家中，只有49%的上小学的年纪的女孩能够上学，而相比于男生为71%。现在据公布的数据为男女比例都为90%。在1998年只有一半的二年级的女孩入学，而现在了有二分之三。在相同的领域上面，女性文盲的比例从五分之一降到了15-24岁的十分之一，使得她们可以与男生相提并论了。

女性小孩比起以前更受欢迎了。在一些国家的父母们甚至更爱女孩。在一些地方，比如中国，因为性别选择而导致的女性胎儿的流产已经成为常态，但是现在往往不那么如此。女孩在孩童时期的结婚也变得很少了。在1995年的南亚，十分之六中的大多数的女孩都在达到18岁的时候就结婚了。这个比例现在已经下降了一半。在整个世界上，这个比例已经从四分之一降到了五分之一。
